Before we tell you about hair loss, let’s understand hair first. Each strand of hair consists of the root called Follicle, which is under the scalp. Above the scalp, the part of the hair that is visible is mostly made of keratin. Keratin is the protein that is left behind from dead cells. It is the same substance that your nails are made of. An average human has between 1,00,000 and 1,50,000 strands of hair. Each strand has a life span of anywhere between 2 to 6 years. Each strand of hair goes through three stages in its life cycle .

Stage one is the growth phase. When each hair follicle receives nutrition and You can see growth of up to 1 cm per month. Most of the hair on people’s head is in the first stage. This stage can last anywhere from 2-6 years. The longer the hair stays in this phase the longer the hair grows. Stage two is when it goes into a resting phase. Nutrition to the strand stops and so does the growth. Stage three is where the resting hair is shed. Once growth stops, cells above the root are shed. It is normal to lose about a 100 to even as much as 200 strands of hair in a day.

When one strand falls off, a new strand of hair grows out of the root. And the cycle continues. The problem that people with hairloss have is that cycle gets interrupted. When one strand falls off, regrowth does not happen as normal, leaving behind bare areas on the scalp. In most cases, areas on top of the head are affected and people can have completely normal hair growth on the sides and at the back of the head

Baldness in men

The most common hair loss in men is what is called Male Pattern Baldness. A hormone called DiHydroTestostrone is known to trigger signals to the hair to get into stage three of its life cycle prematurely. This causes the hair to fall off much earlier than normal. And eventually stop growing completely.

Common causes of hairloss

Even though man has been searching for cures for hair loss since centuries, very little is known about the causes. Hair loss can be attributed to a few commonly known causes.

  • Genetics

    The most common cause is heredity. People are known to carry genes which trigger hair loss. These can be inherited either from their mother’s family or the father’s family. If any of your blood relatives has experienced hair loss, there is a chance you could too.

  • Stress

    Stress is beginning to become one of the biggest causes of hair loss now-a-days. Learning how to cope with daily issues, planning your day ahead etc can help ease stress in your life. Identify issues that are stressing you out and find solutions.


  • Hormonal changes

    Changes in the levels of hormones also induces hair loss. These changes can cause the body to give wrong signals and cause hair to fall off much earlier than what its life span should be.

  • Illness and Treatments

    Issues with thyroid, vitamin deficiencies, anemia etc. also tend to cause hair loss. Some treatments like chemotherapy are also known to induce hair loss.


  • Dietary issues

    Drop in the protein levels can stop hair growth(remember? hair is made of protein). As levels of protein in the body falls, hair fall begins too. Other vitamin deficiencies are also known to cause hair loss.

  • Styling and Hair Products

    Excessive use of hair styling tools like hair straighteners and hair irons dry out hair gradually, making them susceptible to fall. Excessive use of non- natural hair products can also damage hair in the long run. The chemicals in these products cause damage to the hair and over time this would result in hair being unable to grow to its natural length.
